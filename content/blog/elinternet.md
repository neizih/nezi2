Before creating my personal website I saw many others that motivated me to create my own, all of them had one thing in common, they didn't update their blogs section. For many months I would wait for them to publish any new content, but there was nothing. I promised myself to publish regularly once I had my own website, unfortunately I only published one single post in all of 2025.

I've had many topics in my mind that I wanted to write about. However, no matter what or how much I wrote, I deleted the texts the next day as soon as I finished reading them. The thought that people could find my texts embarrassing made me not want to publish any posts at all. That's an absurd way of thinking!

I firmly believe in creating for the sake of creating, so I would like to write a lot of texts, take a lot of pictures and videos among other things to share them on this website. I would love to continue building this page and taking advantage of it to explore my creative side. The alternative of this is to <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doomscrolling" target="_blank">Doomscroll</a> for the rest of my days just to realize that I neither saved my memories nor built something, a bigger shame than the embarrasment I might feel from someone reading my posts. 

The truth is I constantly forget that this page is entirely mine and it's made for me, I limited myself to think that a blog section had to follow certain standard but I'm free to write about me, a story, a current problem, a thought, a particular day, my music tastes, and even teach something. I would like to consider this section an online journal. 

That being said, my texts might still be embarrassing but it's for that exact same reason that I will publish at least one post a month. I will be open to any correction that can help improve my writing. At the same time, I hope I can motivate anyone reading this to develop their own creative ideas and share them in any way. In honor of this, the next post will be about how to create your own personal website.

I'm reading this book called *Bird by Bird — Anne Lamott" that might help improve my writing skills.
